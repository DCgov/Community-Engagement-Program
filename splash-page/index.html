What is it?

This is a tool to gather opinions from residents about their experiences and priorities. Its purpose is to spark conversations between government and residents, starting with a quick survey or poll. People in DC Government then use the results to make decisions, communicate with residents, and drive change. These results are shared transparently so that residents can see how their feedback has influenced decisions and the city.

Government decision makers want public input on a policy or plan, so they do a quick, easy survey of a representative group of residents online, by phone, mail, kiosk, etc.

Empowered with a convenient way to engage, more residents give their feedback and opinions on issues that matter to them.

Results are transparently presented and discussed, in turn informing decision making.

The process of collecting and using resident feedback becomes a regular practice for DC government.

Why does it matter?


Every day, DC government makes decisions that affect how you and other residents experience life in DC. Every resident of DC deserves a voice in these decisions. We aim to collect inclusive, representative feedback so that City decision-makers can be better informed about the residents affected by their decisions.

How can I get involved?


We are early in the development of this idea (we don’t even have a name for it yet!). We are currently testing our ideas about how this new platform will work. There are two easy ways you can get involved:

  1. Sign up to join our mailing list to follow the development of this new program as we take it from idea to practice, and learn about opportunities to shape it along the way.
  2. You may be invited to participate in one or more low-cost test surveys or polls by mail, email, text or web site. Respond to our questions and be heard. We will use the results to make decisions and improve our idea. Here is a link to our current survey.
