DC’s Community Engagement Program




We're building an easier way for residents to make their voices heard and for DC government to listen. Government decision makers ask questions formatted as short surveys and residents across the City, like you, answer them. This is also our big idea for the Bloomberg Mayors Challenge.

What is it?

EngageDC finds people at their doorsteps, mailboxes, communities, phones, computers - Where they are!

Empowered with relevant information, people provide feedback and give their opinions.

Results are transparently presented and discussed, in turn informiing decision-making.

The process of collecting and using resident feedback is instituationalized within DC Government. Agencies are empowered to incorporate resident experience into their work.

Why does it matter?


Every day, DC government makes decisions that affect how you and other residents experience life in DC. Every resident of DC deserves a voice in these decisions. We aim to collect inclusive, representative feedback so that City decision-makers can be better informed about the residents affected by their decisions.

How can I get involved?


We are early in the development of this idea (we don’t even have a name for it yet!). We are currently testing our ideas about how this new platform will work. There are two easy ways you can help:
1) sign up to join our mailing list to follow the development of this new program as we take it from idea to practice, and learn about opportunities to shape it along the way.
2) You may be invited to participate in one or more low-cost test surveys or polls by mail, email, text or web site. Respond to our questions and be heard. We will use the results to make decisions and improve our idea. Here is a link to our current survey.
